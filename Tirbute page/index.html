Dr. Ahmed Zewail

“The father of femtochemistry”

Feb 26, 1946 Ahmed Hassan Zewail was born on February 26, 1946 in the city of Damanhour, Beheira Governorate, and at the age of 4 he moved with his family to the city of Desouq in the Kafr El-Sheikh Governorate, where he grew up and received his basic education there.

1967 He joined the Faculty of Science, Alexandria University after obtaining high school, and obtained a Bachelor of Science with distinction with honors in 1967 in chemistry, and worked as a teaching assistant at the faculty, then obtained a master's degree in research in the science of light.

1974-1976 He traveled to the United States to continue his studies on a scholarship, and received his Ph.D. from the University of Pennsylvania in laser sciences. He then worked as a researcher at the University of California, Berkeley (1974-1976). Then he moved to work at the California Institute of Technology since 1976, which is one of the largest scientific universities in the United States of America.

1982 He obtained American citizenship in 1982. He rose to academic positions within Caltech University until he became a principal professor of chemistry at Caltech, the highest university scientific position in the United States of America, succeeding Linus Pauling, who won the Nobel Prize twice; The first is in chemistry and the second is in world peace.

Oct 21, 1999 Ahmed Zewail was awarded the Nobel Prize in Chemistry for his invention of a camera for spectral analysis that works at femtosecond speed, and his study of chemical reactions using it, thus becoming the first Egyptian and Arab scientist to win the Nobel Prize in Chemistry, and to enter the whole world in a new time that mankind did not expect to realize because of his ability to monitor Movement of atoms within molecules during a chemical reaction by fast laser technology.

1989 Sultan Qaboos Prize in Science and Physics 1989, Sultanate of Oman.

1993 1993 Israeli Wolf Prize in Chemistry.

1998 The Benjamin Franklin Medal in 1998 for his work in the study of chemical reactions in an infinitesimal time (femtoseconds) called femtochemistry.

2009 In April 2009, the White House announced the selection of Dr. Ahmed Zewail on the US President's Council of Advisors for Science and Technology, which includes 20 distinguished scientists in a number of fields.
